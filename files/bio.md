## Full bio

### Background

Daud started his software journey through the ALX Software Engineering Programme (Cohort 17) — one of Africa's most rigorous engineering curricula — completing it over 12 months while simultaneously building client products and delivering real production projects. His foundation spans low-level C programming through to modern web frameworks, giving him an unusually broad technical range for a developer at his stage.

### What I do

Daud builds full-stack web applications, SaaS products, and IoT systems — primarily for clients in Ghana, with growing interest in remote and international work. His clients range from large enterprises (KGL Group, Allied Ghana) to civic institutions (university SRC) to early-stage startups. He handles everything from requirements and architecture through to deployment and ongoing maintenance, often as the sole technical person on a project.

### How I work

Daud's default mode is end-to-end ownership — he takes a brief, figures out the architecture, builds it, ships it, and keeps it running. He's comfortable navigating ambiguous requirements, difficult clients, and production problems. Years of delivering projects solo have sharpened his ability to scope work, manage client relationships, and coordinate with designers, networking engineers, and other collaborators.

### Recent focus

Over the last year, Daud has expanded significantly beyond web development. He built a full IoT laundry kiosk system (hardware integration, Android kiosk app, backend API, admin dashboard) and went deep on DevOps, Linux server administration, and CI/CD pipelines. Most recently he designed and shipped Memraiq — an AI SaaS platform with a custom-built RAG pipeline covering semantic chunking, Qdrant vector search, Neo4j graph retrieval, query classification, and cross-encoder reranking. He built it entirely solo across four services and three live deployments. He's now actively developing the next version, which replaces the initial third-party RAG dependency with his own system.

### Beyond work

Daud is interested in how technology can solve practical, everyday problems in the Ghanaian and broader African context — not just for large organisations, but for small businesses and institutions that rarely get purpose-built software.
