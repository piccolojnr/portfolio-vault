## The problem

KGL Group — a major Ghanaian conglomerate with six subsidiaries spanning Fintech, Logistics, Agric, Property Development, and Commerce — needed a corporate website that reflected their scale and position. They had a design ready via Paper Merchant (a brand solutions agency) but needed a developer to implement it fully, integrate a CMS for ongoing content management, and deliver a production-ready site.

---

## What I did

- Implemented the full website from a Figma design provided by Paper Merchant — pixel-accurate, no design input from my side
- Built multi-page navigation covering all six subsidiaries, careers, press releases, gallery, and sponsorships sections
- Integrated Sanity CMS so the client can manage content independently after handover
- Deployed to production and handed over a fully functioning site with CMS access
- Delivered within approximately 2 months
- Quality of work led directly to a referral — Allied Ghana came through the same Paper Merchant relationship

---

## How I did it

**Tools & technologies:**
- Next.js
- Sanity CMS
- TypeScript
- Tailwind CSS

**Approach / methodology:**

Implementation-focused project — the design direction was fully defined by Paper Merchant's Figma file. My job was accurate, performant implementation with a CMS layer that a non-technical team could use for ongoing updates. Used Next.js for performance and SSG/SSR capabilities, Sanity for structured content management across the multi-page site.

## What made this hard

- Large multi-page site with many distinct content types — careers, press, gallery, subsidiaries, sponsorships all needed separate CMS schemas
- Maintaining pixel accuracy across a complex Figma design at production quality
- Setting up Sanity CMS schemas to match the content model across a multi-section site so the client could self-manage after handover

---

## What I'm proud of

Earning the referral. The KGL Group project came in, was delivered cleanly and on time, and the same agency introduced us to Allied Ghana without us having to ask. That's the best signal that the work was genuinely good.

---

## What I'd do differently

I'd establish a clearer content handover process earlier — documenting which sections are CMS-managed vs hardcoded would have saved time during the final handover and training phase.
